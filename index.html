In 1975 people left their homes unlocked, their keys in the ignition when they parked up town, and importantly when you passed someone on the road you waved.

There were no B & Bâ€™s and no motels. In the evenings and on weekends if you came across someone, you knew that they either lived here or were visiting someone who did.

Everybody waved because you knew that the person you were passing was a neighbor or at the least a fellow Islander, temporary or permanent. There is an energy that passes between people when you wave. You see each other. You are joined at the hip in a way. That gets us through tough times, enlivens our souls, and makes us extended family.

Vashon Island is encircled by Waves from the ocean, blanketed by radio Waves from Voice of Vashon, so it is natural that we Wave.

If we stop waving to each other we will lose our sense of community.
